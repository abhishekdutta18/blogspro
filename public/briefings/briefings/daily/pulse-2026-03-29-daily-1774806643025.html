Institutional capital rotation accelerates as Fed signals termination of the tightening cycle, triggering a significant systemic shift in the macro-economic landscape.

The current macro-economic landscape reflects a significant systemic shift, driven by the Federal Reserve's decision to terminate the tightening cycle. This has led to a surge in institutional capital rotation, with investors seeking to capitalize on the emerging trends. According to a report by Yahoo Finance, the yield on the 10-year Treasury note has declined to 4.25%, indicating a bearish outlook for emerging market currencies.
