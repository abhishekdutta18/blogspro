Global Macro Drift

Institutional capital rotation is underway as the Fed signals termination of the tightening cycle, triggering a significant systemic shift in the macro-economic landscape.

The current macro-economic landscape reflects a significant systemic shift, driven by the Federal Reserve's decision to terminate the tightening cycle. This has triggered a wave of institutional capital rotation, with investors seeking to reposition their portfolios in anticipation of a potential economic downturn. Strong analyst sentiment on NICE amid AI push acceleration has led to a surge in investor interest, with the stock experiencing a significant uptick in recent trading sessions, according to YAHOO_FINANCE.

The DXY Index, a key benchmark for the US dollar, has been consolidating at 104.2, indicating a bearish outlook for emerging market currencies. The US 10Y Yield has been drifting upwards to 4.25%, resulting in tightening credit spreads. The yield curve has flattened significantly, indicating a potential recessionary environment, BLOOMBERG notes. The FII inflow into India has been robust, with a weekly inflow of $2.4B, providing liquidity support to the Nifty. The India GDP growth rate has been estimated at 7.2% by institutional sources, which may lead to a sovereign premium hike.

The sentiment score, which measures the level of fear and greed in the market, has been hovering at 9, indicating extreme fear. Gold has surged to $4493.69, representing a 2.64% increase. The US dollar has also appreciated against the Indian rupee, with the exchange rate standing at 94.76, representing a 0.54% increase. REUTERS reports that the recent surge in gold prices has been driven by safe-haven demand.
