Tail Risks and Liquidity Friction


The 2026-2027 horizon is characterized by extreme structural fragility, with tail risks and liquidity friction dominating market dynamics. A gamma squeeze is expected to occur, driven by institutional investors' attempts to hedge against potential losses. This will lead to a sharp increase in implied volatility, exacerbating liquidity friction and further amplifying market volatility.


Regulatory Headwinds


Regulatory headwinds will also play a significant role in shaping market dynamics. The ongoing review of financial regulations is expected to lead to increased scrutiny of institutional investors, further limiting their ability to engage in market manipulation. This will result in a decrease in market liquidity, making it increasingly difficult for investors to exit positions.


Institutional Consolidation


Institutional consolidation is expected to occur as a result of the gamma squeeze and regulatory friction. Large institutional investors will be forced to consolidate their positions, leading to a decrease in market liquidity and an increase in market volatility. This will create opportunities for smaller investors to enter the market, but also increases the risk of market crashes.
